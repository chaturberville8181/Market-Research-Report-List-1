<p><p>The rigid mechanical couplings market consists of three primary types: sleeve, flanged, and clamped couplings. Sleeve couplings connect two shafts end-to-end using a cylindrical sleeve, ensuring alignment and torque transfer. Flanged couplings feature two flanges bolted together, facilitating easier installation and disassembly. Clamped couplings utilize a clamp mechanism to securely join shafts, allowing for quick adjustments and maintenance. Each type serves to transmit torque and maintain shaft alignment in various industrial applications, enhancing machinery efficiency and reliability.</p></p>
