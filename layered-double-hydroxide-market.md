<p><p>Layered Double Hydroxides (LDHs) are anionic clay materials characterized by a unique layered structure, where positively charged metal hydroxide layers alternate with interlayer anions. Several factors contribute to this growth, including increasing demand for eco-friendly materials in the construction and packaging industries. The rise of nanotechnology and the exploration of new applications in energy storage devices, like batteries and supercapacitors, are further propelling market expansion. </p><p>Emerging trends also encompass the development of hybrid and composite materials that leverage the unique properties of LDHs, enhancing their applicability across various industries.
