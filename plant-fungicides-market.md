<p><p>The plant fungicides market includes various types that cater to different plant diseases and agricultural needs. Dithiocarbamates are broad-spectrum protectants, while benzimidazoles target specific pathogens. SDHI (Succinate Dehydrogenase Inhibitors) disrupt fungal respiration, and phenylamides focus on oomycetes. Strobilurins offer systemic protection with preventive and curative properties, whereas triazoles inhibit ergosterol biosynthesis in fungi. Bio-fungicides use natural organisms to combat pathogens, promoting sustainable practices. The "Others" category encompasses emerging and niche fungicides not classified in the primary groups.</p></p>
