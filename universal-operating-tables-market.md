<p><p>The Universal Operating Tables market is divided into two main types: motorized and non-motorized tables. Motorized operating tables feature electric or hydraulic mechanisms that allow for easy adjustments of height, tilt, and positioning, enhancing surgical precision and efficiency. In contrast, non-motorized tables rely on manual adjustments, often requiring more physical effort from the surgical team. While motorized tables offer greater convenience and versatility, non-motorized options are typically more cost-effective and easier to maintain, catering to different healthcare facility needs.</p></p>
